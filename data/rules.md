📜 Rules and Regulations for Loyalty Programs in India

Loyalty programs in India are governed by a complex framework of laws and regulations. Below is a structured overview of the key regulations.

🔐 1. Data Protection & Privacy (Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023)

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) is the most significant recent regulation impacting loyalty programs in India.

Consent Requirements

Prior, Informed Consent: Businesses can no longer rely on opaque or blanket consents. Instead, consent must be informed, specific, and revocable.
Clear Notice: Under Section 5, a clear notice must be displayed detailing what data is collected, for what purpose, and the customer's rights. The notice must be in English or any of the 22 scheduled Indian languages.
No Pre-Ticked Boxes: Section 6 prohibits obtaining consent through pre-ticked boxes or bundled agreements.
Data Principles

Purpose Limitation: Data collected for one purpose cannot be repurposed without fresh consent. Retention without clarity becomes a compliance risk.
Children's Data: Under Section 9, processing children's data (persons below 18 years of age) requires verified parental consent.
Rights and Obligations

Right to Withdraw: Customers have the right to withdraw consent at any time, and such withdrawal must be made easy (Section 6(4)).
Exit Process: Customers cannot be blocked from exiting the program, though consequences like loss of accumulated points may be disclosed.
Grievance Redressal: Programs must establish clear mechanisms for handling customer complaints and data-related grievances.
For Significant Data Fiduciaries (Large Retailers)

Under Section 10, Large Retailers classified as Significant Data Fiduciaries must:

Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO)
Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
Perform regular audits
Penalties

Breach Notification: Section 8(6) requires mandatory breach notification to affected customers and the regulator.
Penalty: Up to ₹200 crore for failure to notify.
💳 2. Financial Regulations (RBI Guidelines)

For loyalty programs involving bank cards, the RBI Master Directions on Credit Card and Debit Card Issuance and Conduct Directions, 2022 apply (effective July 1, 2022).

Definition

"Card Loyalty / Reward Programme" is defined as schemes linked to a debit/credit card where the card-issuer or associated merchants offer digital coupons, points, discounts, cashbacks, or other benefits having monetary value that can be redeemed for current or future transactions.

Governance Requirements

Board-Approved Policy: Each card-issuer must have a well-documented, board-approved policy for card issuance and conduct, published on their website.
Half-Yearly Review: Card-issuers must have a mechanism for half-yearly review of card operations by the Audit Committee, covering customer service, frauds, complaints, and grievance redressal.
Key Fact Statement (KFS)

Card-issuers must provide a one-page Key Fact Statement containing key aspects of the card, including rate of interest, quantum of charges, and Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC).

Fraud & Unfair Trade Practices

Fintech loyalty programs must comply with the Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices Regulations (FUTP), particularly Regulation 4(2)(d), which prohibits manipulation of securities prices through offering "any money or money's worth".

AMFI Code for Mutual Funds

For mutual fund-related loyalty programs, the AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India) Code of Conduct (Clauses 9, 13, and 14) prohibits rebates or incentives used to lure investors into additional purchases.

🛡️ 3. Consumer Protection Laws

Consumer Protection Act, 2019

Loyalty programs are subject to the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, and the rules issued thereunder:

No False Representation: Businesses cannot misrepresent themselves or their offers to consumers.
Unfair Trade Practices: Loyalty programs with misleading terms, hidden conditions, or deceptive marketing may be deemed unfair trade practices under the Act.
Specific Requirements

MITC: The Most Important Terms and Conditions (MITC) must be clearly communicated at the time of enrollment, defining responsibilities and liabilities of both the program operator and the customer.
Transparency: Loyalty programs cannot have hidden conditions or change rules unilaterally without notice.
💰 4. Tax Implications

Goods & Services Tax (GST)

Input Tax Credit (ITC) on Rewards:

The Myntra AAR/AAAR rulings have held that vouchers given to customers on redemption of loyalty points are treated as "free of cost" and amount to disposal by way of gift.
As a result, Input Tax Credit (ITC) is denied under Section 17(5)(h) of the CGST Act for vouchers and subscription packages procured for loyalty programs.
Vouchers have been held to be "neither goods nor services," so no GST is chargeable, and consequently, ITC does not arise.
GST on Points Redemption:

GST is levied on the total price of the product, not the discounted value after adjusting loyalty points.
Experts suggest that ideally, GST should be paid on the amount after reducing the sum attributable to loyalty points if conditions are met.
Income Tax

For Customers (Individuals):

Generally Non-Taxable: In most cases, rewards earned from point-based programs are treated as non-taxable rather than actual income. This includes merchandise, gift cards, travel miles, and automatic cashback.
High-Value Rewards: High-value cash rewards or contest prizes may require tax documentation and could be treated as taxable income.
Sign-Up Bonuses: These typically avoid taxation as they require purchases rather than direct monetary awards.
Caution: Members should consult a chartered accountant, as the Income Tax Act could classify some rewards as "Income from Other Sources," necessitating tax payment.
For Businesses:

No maximum limit has been prescribed beyond which loyalty benefits must be declared as taxable income under the Income Tax Act.
📝 5. Contract Law Framework

Indian Contract Act, 1872

Loyalty programs are fundamentally governed by contract law:

Consideration: Loyalty points represent a form of consideration where the customer's purchases are exchanged for future rewards.
Enforceability: The terms and conditions of loyalty programs must meet the requirements of a valid contract (offer, acceptance, lawful consideration, intention to create legal relations).
Restrictive Clauses

Under Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872, any agreement that restrains a person from exercising a lawful profession, trade, or business is void. This may impact loyalty programs that attempt to lock customers into exclusive arrangements through restrictive terms.
⚖️ 6. Other Applicable Regulations

E-Commerce Rules

Under the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020 (as amended), e-commerce platforms must establish grievance redressal mechanisms and comply with display and labelling criteria. Loyalty programs on e-commerce platforms must adhere to these rules.

Industry-Specific Regulations

Different sectors may have additional regulations (e.g., aviation for frequent flyer programs, hospitality for hotel loyalty programs, banking for reward points).

🛠️ Practical Compliance Checklist for Loyalty Programs

Area	Requirement
Enrollment	Layered notices, separate opt-ins for marketing, clear purpose disclosure
Data Management	Obtain informed consent, enable withdrawal of consent, implement purpose limitation
Governance	Board-approved policy for banks/NBFCs, half-yearly reviews
Terms & Conditions	MITC provided, no hidden conditions, published on website
Exit Process	Allow customers to exit; disclose consequences (e.g., loss of points)
Partner Alignment	Contractually bind vendors/banks to honour consent signals
Tax Compliance	Assess GST on full value, consult CA on income tax implications
Grievance Redressal	Robust complaint handling mechanism
Breach Notification	Mandatory notification to affected customers and regulator; penalty up to ₹200 crore
